3. Commodities vs Stocks — The Key Differences

If your asset is a commodity (gold, oil, copper, or commodity ETFs like GLD/USO/DBC), a big slice of the library doesn't apply. Knowing why up front saves time.

Why fundamental strategies don't work on commodities

Gold doesn't have earnings. There's no balance sheet, no P/E, no buyback yield, no analyst revisions. Strategies that rank by fundamentals (Value Composite, Piotroski Quality, Buyback Yield, PEAD, Earnings Revisions) are mechanically incompatible. The wizard skips them automatically for commodities.

Why trend / breakout strategies shine on commodities

Supply shocks (OPEC, droughts, mining outages) and macro narratives (inflation, dollar weakness) create persistent trends. Time-series momentum and Donchian breakout have been profitable on commodities for decades — this is the CTA / managed-futures playbook applied to single names.

Why mean-reversion can work on commodities

When inventories are well-supplied and there's no shock, commodities trade in ranges. Bollinger Bands and RSI work, especially on industrial metals and ag commodities. Don't combine with high leverage — the regime can flip violently to trend mode.

Why news sentiment is noisier

Commodity news is heavily macro-driven (Fed, OPEC, China growth). Single-commodity sentiment scores from NLP models are less precise than for individual stocks. If you use it, combine with a trend co-signal.

Practical commodity stack for retail. Most retail-friendly commodity strategies on Livermore: Moving Average Filter, Time-Series Momentum, Breakout, Bollinger Mean-Reversion. Add the Vol-Target overlay to keep position sizes reasonable when realized volatility spikes.

4. Tie-Breakers — When Two Strategies Look Equally Good

The wizard will sometimes return three strategies with similar scores. Use these five questions, in this order, to pick:

#QuestionWhy it mattersHow to act
1Which has more historical evidence?Tier A (decades of replication) beats Tier B (recent / capacity-limited) beats Tier C (mixed).Prefer Tier A unless the asset is exotic.
2Does your monitoring rhythm match?A monthly-rebalance strategy run by someone who checks daily creates noise; a daily strategy run by someone who checks monthly creates missed exits.Pick the strategy whose natural cadence matches your check-in habit.
3What's the tax footprint?High-turnover strategies (Short-Term Reversal, News Sentiment) realize short-term gains, taxed as income.In a taxable account, prefer lower-turnover strategies. In an IRA / 401(k), ignore.
4Sleep test — how big a drop can you sit through?Cross-Sectional Momentum has had −30% drawdowns in months. Low-Volatility has had −10%. Same return doesn't mean same comfort.If > 20% peak-to-trough drawdown ruins your sleep, prefer the lower-DD option.
5Are you trying to learn, or to deploy?Simpler templates (MA Filter, Breakout) teach you about price; composite templates (Multi-Factor) are black-boxier.If learning, prefer the simplest of the three. If deploying, prefer the most robust.
If after all five tie-breakers you still can't decide, the answer is "run both small — on paper or with $1k each — for one cycle, then double-down on the one that feels right." Forced comparison teaches more than analysis.

5. Build Your Own — The Simplified 4-Block Model

Every strategy in the Livermore library is a combination of four building blocks. Once you see this, the library isn't 20+ separate things; it's a handful of choices stacked together. You can mix-and-match to build your own.

1
WHAT
The asset(s) you'll trade
  • Just one ticker (e.g. AAPL)
  • A small basket (5–15 names)
  • A pair of related assets
  • Sector ETFs (the 11 SPDRs)
  • A broad market ETF (SPY, VTI)
2
WHEN IN
What triggers you to buy
  • Trend filter (price > moving average)
  • Momentum (top of basket by N-month return)
  • Mean reversion (RSI < 30, or below lower band)
  • Breakout (new N-day high)
  • Fundamental (top decile of factor score)
  • Signal (insider buying, sentiment turn)
3
HOW MUCH
Position sizing & risk
  • Full position (one ticker)
  • Equal weight across selected names
  • Vol target (auto-scale to 10% annual vol)
  • Hard stop at −X% from entry
  • Combined: equal-weight + per-name stop
4
WHEN OUT
What triggers you to sell
  • Entry signal flips (symmetric)
  • Mean-reversion target (RSI > 70, back to MA)
  • Fixed holding period (5d, 1m, 1q)
  • Hit stop loss
  • Periodic rebalance (re-rank monthly)

Building your own = pick one option from each of the four blocks. Most retail-friendly custom strategies are just a small twist on a library template — e.g. "Sector Rotation, but with the Vol-Target overlay" or "Pairs Trading, but on commodity ETFs (GLD/SLV instead of stock pairs)".

Three retail-friendly custom recipes to try

Recipe A: "Buy SPY when it's safe"

WHAT: SPY only. IN: Price > 200-day MA. SIZE: Full. OUT: Price < 200-day MA. Sidesteps 2008 / 2022-style bear markets.

Recipe B: "Defensive Gold Trend"

WHAT: GLD. IN: 12-month return > 0 AND price > 200-day MA. SIZE: Vol-target 10%. OUT: Either signal flips. Catches gold rallies, exits during chops.

Recipe C: "Sector Top-3"

WHAT: 11 SPDR sector ETFs. IN: Top-3 by 6-month return. SIZE: Equal-weight 1/3 each. OUT: Monthly re-rank. Robust default for prosumer users.

6. Notes for the Livermore Product Team

How to wire this picker into the actual product.

6.1 Where this lives in the user journey

  • Onboarding — new user lands on the template gallery and sees a "Not sure where to start? Try the picker." CTA that opens this wizard.
  • Stuck-user assist — if a user has run the chat builder for 5+ minutes without converging on a strategy, offer the wizard.
  • Education tab — link this page from any tooltip that says "what kind of strategy is this?"

6.2 Wizard implementation in Livermore

The wizard is pure HTML+JS and self-contained. To embed in the Next.js app:

  1. Port the question/strategy data structure into apps/web/src/lib/strategy-picker/data.ts.
  2. Build a React component StrategyPicker at apps/web/src/components/strategy-picker/.
  3. Recommendations link directly to the existing template gallery cards by livermore_strategy_type.
  4. Track picker_started, picker_completed, picker_template_selected in template_usage_events so the quarterly cycle can see which questions are most predictive.

6.3 What this enables

This picker is the bridge from the universe of strategies (the Strategy Library) to the specific recommendation the user actually clicks. It also gives retail users a mental model of how the templates relate so they can graduate to the chat builder or the 4-block custom builder when ready.

6.4 Telemetry to add to the next quarterly cycle

  • Picker completion rate (% who answer all 5 questions)
  • Most common answer combinations
  • Click-through rate from recommendation → backtest
  • Which recommendations are never selected (candidates for removal from the wizard pool)
